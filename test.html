Vintage Car Rally

Classic rallying, or historic rallying, is a type of road rally suitable for most standard classic cars, with no special equipment needed (the equipment allowed depends on the particular rally). These rallies are more about enjoyment than speed, and can be a good introduction to historic motorsport (which also include race meetings, classic endurance, and hillclimbing). In a vintage car rally, participants compete on their skills, not on speed. A classic car rally is a race where participants follow the traffic rules and can enjoy driving with their beloved car, and at the same time it entertains the eyes of roadside spectators. The idea of a rally is to travel from a point to another within a certain time , not too fast or too slow, trying to match a speed average set by the organizers.

BMW 1972

Introduced in May 1972, the BMW 1972 was a homologation special built to make the car eligible for racing in the European Touring Car Championship. The BMW 1972 is the first generation of 5 Series executive cars, which was produced from 1972 to 1981 and replaced the saloon models of the BMW New Class range.

Brand: BMW
Model: CSL
First release year: 1972

CHEVROLET

The Chevrolet El Camino is a coupé utility vehicle that was produced by Chevrolet between 1959–60 and 1964–1987. Unlike a standard pickup truck, the El Camino was adapted from the standard two-door Chevrolet station wagon platform and integrated the cab and cargo bed into the body. The Chevrolet El Camino coupe utility vehicle was classified as an SUV at the time. It was based on the chassis of a sedan but offered a sizeable bed behind the seats. The third generation El Camino was the second to last to be based on the Chevelle platform.

Brand: Chevrolet
Model: El Camino SS
First release year: 1970
